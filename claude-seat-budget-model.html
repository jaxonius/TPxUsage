The strategy why this shape

The seat is cheap; the upgrade is the lever. AI usage is a power law — a handful of power users each return many times their seat cost, while a long tail barely logs in. 50 Standard seats = $12K/yr; 50 Premium = $60K/yr. So the whole budget question is how many people earn the 5× Premium upgrade — and Phase 1 answers that with data, not guesswork. You likely won't spend all $60K, and that's correct — hold the gap for the 1–2 extreme-tail users (raw usage-based headroom) and expansion.

Vehicle: Claude Team (mix Standard + Premium, 5–150 seats, SSO/SCIM/analytics). Not Enterprise — it unbundles tokens, so 50 active devs run ~$150–250/dev/mo in usage on top of seats (toggle the comparison above). Enterprise only wins if per-user spend caps / audit / CMEK are mandatory.

Phase 1 — Broad baseline · wks 1–6

All 50 on Standard (~$12K/yr; ~$48K uncommitted). Usage analytics on, shared CLAUDE.md, managers actively encourage use (7× more daily users). Don't judge before ~wk 4 — ramp is real.

Phase 2 — Tier & upgrade · the reassessment

Trigger: someone hits their Standard limit or aggregate pace climbs. Upgrade those users to Premium (+$960/yr each); reclaim dormant seats. Expect 60–70% weekly-active at best.

Phase 3 — Concentrate & guardrail · month 4+

Push budget to the proven tail; reserve for outliers beyond Premium. Assign a permanent owner (~10% of one engineer) running a monthly reclaim-and-redeploy cadence; keep dormancy <10%.

Measure value honestly

Anchor decisions to weekly-active % + PR throughput + time-saved/DXI moving together, computed per tier, not per head. Distrust vanity metrics (lines-of-code, "% AI-generated", monthly-active) and self-reports — the METR RCT found experienced devs were 19% slower with AI yet believed they were ~20% faster. Track change-failure-rate alongside speed (2025 DORA: AI lifts throughput but hurts stability without strong tests/CI).
